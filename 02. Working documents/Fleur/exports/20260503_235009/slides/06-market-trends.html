Belastingdienst’s AI agenda sits at the intersection of workforce scarcity, legal scrutiny and sovereign technology constraints

Three named forces shape the deployable AI agenda

Workforce pressure

  • Tight labor, retirement outflow and shrinking budgets constrain capacity.
  • Rising complexity increases pressure on delivery.

AI acceleration

  • Generative AI and agentic use cases are developing rapidly.
  • The client’s strategy framing puts these use cases on the agenda.

Deployment constraints

  • AI Act, AVG, Awb, Archiefwet, BIO and cloud policy shape deployment.
  • Sovereignty, electricity capacity, vendor lock-in and non-European dependence are concerns.

What this means for Belastingdienst

  • Select use cases that address labor pressure, budgets and rising complexity.
  • Design deployments around legal, records, security and cloud-policy constraints.
  • Manage sovereignty, electricity capacity, lock-in and non-European dependence from the start.
  • Favor open source where possible and controlled cloud adoption.

Source: Client materials and strategy framing
