## 7. Approach

**Working principles**

- **Executive-led** — decisions owned by Philips leadership; we provide structure, facts, options, and **transparent** valuation logic.  
- **Iterative** — weekly steering touchpoints [TBD cadence]; scenario and strategy choices pressure-tested in workshops.  
- **RFP-traceable** — each major deliverable maps to the RFP elements in §1.

**Workstreams (RFP-aligned)**

1. **Market trends & competitive landscape** — integrated fact base and implications (feeds all downstream work).  
2. **Scenario design** — select **scenario axes** [TBD with client], build **2–4** [TBD] futures to 2035, define **signposts** and implications for Philips.  
3. **Strategic moves** — longlist → shortlist of **potential** moves (organic, inorganic, portfolio, operating model, capital allocation); link each move to scenarios where it creates or destroys value.  
4. **Three strategy packages** — converged narratives: each package = **coherent** set of where-to-play / how-to-win choices + move portfolio + capability implications.  
5. **Share price impact (per strategy)** — agreed **valuation architecture** [TBD], e.g.:  
   - **DCF / residual income** view of corporate cash flows under strategy-specific assumptions; and/or  
   - **Sum-of-the-parts** where relevant businesses are valued with strategy-specific multiples or growth paths;  
   - **Relative** view vs. base case (e.g. % upside/downside to a reference case) — **all numbers [TBD]** until Philips provides forecasts, capital structure, and segment economics.  
   - **Sensitivity** bands on key drivers (growth, margin, WACC, multiple) — ranges [TBD].  
6. **Synthesis & handover** — board-ready storyline comparing the three strategies on **strategic** and **value** dimensions; decision log; [TBD] next-phase implications.
