Slide 2 — Context and Objectives
2 McKinsey & Company

TenneT's board faces three intertwined imperatives that demand a strategy-anchored leadership journey

The situation

  • TenneT, the Dutch-German TSO, sits at the centre of the NL/DE energy transition and must govern an unprecedented multi-decade capex programme.
  • The board operates across two regulatory regimes (ACM in NL, BNetzA in DE) with diverging tariff methodologies and political contexts.
  • The unresolved TenneT Germany ownership question and ongoing top-team turnover compress decision velocity at a moment that demands the opposite.
  • Three stated board-level objectives frame the mandate: energy-transition leadership, operational efficiency, and cross-border governance.

Our day-one answer

An Aberkyn-led executive-board journey, wired into McKinsey's energy and TSO sector spine, will equip the board to govern at a new altitude — connecting personal and collective leadership work directly to grid build-out, cross-border governance, and the ownership decisions in front of them.

Without a deliberate, strategy-anchored journey, board decision velocity and top-team cohesion will not keep pace with the capex programme, putting delivery of the Dutch and German energy transition at risk.
