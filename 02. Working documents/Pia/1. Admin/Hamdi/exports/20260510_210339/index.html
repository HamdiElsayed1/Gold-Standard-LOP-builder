Belastingdienst needs to accelerate responsible AI and digital transformation fast enough to redesign its generic bezwaarproces for scale, legality, and service quality, without creating compliance or delivery risk; a winning response builds the AI foundation and a future-proof bezwaar process that is scalable, AI-enabled, and legally robust.

Slide 2 — Context and Objectives
2 McKinsey & Company

Belastingdienst must turn AI ambition into controlled delivery while modernizing bezwaar under staffing, compliance, and service pressures

The situation

  • RFP IUC26-011 seeks a strategic full-service partner to accelerate responsible AI and digital transformation, with EUR 5.0 mln ceiling over 12 months.
  • Six prioritized AI use cases are in scope alongside building the AI foundation; generative AI today is still largely experimental on sensitive taxpayer data.
  • The first sub-assignment is redesign of the generic bezwaarproces, which must respect Awb deadlines and hearing rights while scaling under peak load.
  • Delivery context is constrained by labour scarcity, an ageing workforce, ICT modernization, and rising citizen expectations on service quality.
Our day-one answer

Run one disciplined programme that builds the responsible AI foundation and redesigns bezwaar in parallel, so the process becomes scalable, AI-enabled, and legally robust under Awb, AI Act, and AVG, without adding compliance or delivery risk.

If we do not deliver this

The Belastingdienst stays constrained by labour shortages, bezwaar bottlenecks, and fragmented AI experimentation, putting statutory deadlines and citizen service quality at risk.

Source: RFP IUC26-011 and Vision on AI 2025-2030; ContextDoc web search (Jaarplan Belastingdienst 2026); team analysis

2026 AUG

EU AI Act high-risk obligations become operational

Transparency and high-risk requirements take effect in August 2026; Dutch guidance confirms government GenAI use must comply with the AI Act and GDPR.

2026 FEB

Bezwaarproces redesign is already a live internal programme

A public Belastingdienst vacancy for a programmamanager herontwerp bezwaarproces names AI as a central lever, signalling an organised redesign track.

2026 JAN

Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens scrutiny on Belastingdienst applications

January 2026 government publication records AP correspondence on locally developed applications, underlining supervisory attention to the application landscape.

Treat the AI foundation and bezwaar redesign as one governance problem, not two separate tracks.

Design every AI use case with human oversight, logging and transparency controls from day one.

Align the architecture with rijksbrede cloud, data and open-source standards to avoid bespoke lock-in.

Build for peak bezwaar volumes under labour scarcity, safeguarding Awb deadlines and service quality.
